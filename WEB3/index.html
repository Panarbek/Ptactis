Planets

Venus

Venus is often referred to as Earth's "sister planet" due to its similar size and composition. It is the second closest planet to Earth, with an average distance of about 41 million kilometers (25 million miles).

Neptune

Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun and is also classified as an ice giant. It is located at an average distance of about 4.4 billion kilometers (2.7 billion miles) from Earth when it is closest in its orbit.

Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and is the smallest planet in our solar system. It is the third closest planet to Earth, with an average distance of about 77 million kilometers (48 million miles).

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system and is known for its prominent gas giant features. It is the fifth planet from the Sun and is located at an average distance of about 628 million kilometers (390 million miles) from Earth.

Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and is famous for its stunning ring system. It is located at an average distance of about 1.2 billion kilometers (746 million miles) from Earth when it is closest in its orbit.

Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and is classified as an ice giant. It is located at an average distance of about 2.9 billion kilometers (1.8 billion miles) from Earth when it is closest in its orbit.
